Your Buying Guide

Everything you need to know about buying a home in British Columbia and across Canada — from pre-approval to closing day.

Step 1: Get Pre-Approved

Before you start looking, get a mortgage pre-approval from a Canadian lender. This tells you exactly what you can afford and shows sellers you're serious. Pre-approvals typically lock in a rate for 90-120 days.

Tip: Use our Mortgage Calculator to estimate your payments based on current rates.

Step 2: Define Your Criteria

Location, property type, beds/baths, budget range, and must-have features. Our platform scores every listing with a transparent deal engine so you can see exactly how each property ranks against the market.

RAG Advantage: Our deal scoring engine compares $/sqft against area medians, flags price drops, and surfaces below-market opportunities automatically.

Step 3: Search & Tour

Browse verified listings on our platform, shortlist properties, and schedule tours. Every listing you see has passed our verification path and source reconciliation. You'll know the data quality and any source discrepancies upfront.

Step 4: Make an Offer

Your agent will help you craft a competitive offer based on comparable sales, market conditions, and the property's deal score. In BC, offers are typically presented through the listing agent with standard BCREA forms.

Step 5: Subjects & Inspection

Most BC offers include subject conditions — typically financing, home inspection, and title review. You'll have a set period (usually 5-10 business days) to complete due diligence before removing subjects.

Step 6: Close & Move In

Once subjects are removed, your lawyer/notary handles the title transfer and your lender advances funds. In BC, you'll also pay the Property Transfer Tax (1% on first $200K, 2% on $200K-$2M, 3% above $2M). First-time buyers may qualify for exemptions.
