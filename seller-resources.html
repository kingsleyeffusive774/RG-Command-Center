Your Selling Guide

Data-backed strategies to price, market, and close the sale of your home in British Columbia and across Canada.

Step 1: Know Your Market Position

Before listing, understand where your property sits relative to the market. Our deal scoring engine benchmarks $/sqft against area medians, identifies comparable sales, and calculates your property's competitive position — the same data buyers on our platform see.

RAG Advantage: We use real municipal assessment data and multi-source reconciliation to give you the most accurate market picture — not just MLS estimates.

Step 2: Price Strategically

Overpricing leads to stale listings and eventual price drops — both of which our signal engine flags for buyers. Pricing at or slightly below market creates urgency and competitive offers. Your agent will use comparable sales data from our platform to find the optimal list price.

Step 3: Prepare Your Home

Declutter, deep clean, and handle minor repairs. Consider staging — staged homes sell faster and for more. Focus on curb appeal, kitchen, and bathrooms. Professional photography is non-negotiable in today's market.

Step 4: Market Aggressively

Your listing will be promoted through the RAG platform, MLS, social media, targeted ads, and our direct buyer network. Our signal engine automatically surfaces your listing to matched buyers based on their criteria and budget.

Step 5: Receive & Negotiate Offers

Review offers with your agent — not just price, but conditions, deposit, closing date, and subject timelines. In competitive BC markets, multiple offer situations are common. Your agent will guide negotiation strategy based on market data.

Step 6: Close the Sale

Once you accept an offer and subjects are removed, your lawyer/notary handles conveyancing. The buyer's lender advances funds on closing day. Be prepared for costs including real estate commission, legal fees, potential capital gains (for investment properties), and any outstanding property taxes.

Seller Checklist Before Listing

✓ Get a pre-listing home inspection to avoid surprises
✓ Order a title search to check for encumbrances
✓ Gather disclosure documents (Property Disclosure Statement in BC)
✓ Review strata documents if applicable (Form B, minutes, depreciation report)
✓ Declutter, clean, and stage the property
✓ Professional photography and virtual tour
✓ Review comparable sales with your agent
✓ Set a competitive list price based on market data
